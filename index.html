English Grammar

Verb Tenses (Present, Past, Future)

Understanding how to express actions in different time frames is crucial. Start with the simple present (for habits and facts), simple past (for completed actions), and simple future (for plans and predictions).

Sentence Structure (Subject-Verb-Object)

Grasping the basic word order of English sentences helps you construct clear and understandable statements. Practice identifying the subject (who or what is doing the action), the verb (the action itself), and the object (who or what receives the action).

Articles (a, an, the)

Knowing when and how to use articles is essential for accurate noun usage. "A/an" is for general, non-specific nouns, while "the" is for specific nouns or those previously mentioned.

Nouns (Singular, Plural, Countable, Uncountable)

Learn the rules for making nouns plural (usually adding "-s" or "-es"), and understand the difference between countable (things you can count, like apples) and uncountable nouns (things you can't count individually, like water).

Pronouns (Subject, Object, Possessive)

Mastering pronoun usage helps you avoid repetition and make your sentences flow smoothly. Learn the different types of pronouns and their roles in a sentence (e.g., "I," "me," "my," "mine").

Adjectives and Adverbs

These words add detail and description to your language. Adjectives modify nouns (e.g., "beautiful flower"), while adverbs modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs (e.g., "She sings beautifully").
