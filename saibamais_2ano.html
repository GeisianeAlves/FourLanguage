Unit 7: Gathering and Sharing Information

Asking and Answering Questions

Asking questions is a fundamental part of communication. To gather information, use question words like:

  • Who: Refers to people (e.g., "Who is your favorite author?")
  • What: Refers to things, actions, or ideas (e.g., "What is your favorite movie?")
  • Where: Refers to places (e.g., "Where are you from?")
  • When: Refers to time (e.g., "When did you start learning English?")
  • Why: Refers to reasons (e.g., "Why do you like to travel?")
  • How: Refers to manner or method (e.g., "How do you make pancakes?")

To answer questions, you can use phrases like:

  • "My favorite author is..."
  • "I started learning English when I was..."
  • "I like to travel because..."
  • "To make pancakes, you need to..."

Discussing Personal Information and Preferences

When getting to know someone, you can ask about their:

  • Name
  • Age
  • Nationality
  • Occupation
  • Hobbies
  • Favorite foods, movies, books, etc.

Be sure to share your own information as well!

Talking about News, Events, and Current Affairs

Stay informed about what's happening in the world by discussing current events. You can use phrases like:

  • "Did you hear about...?"
  • "What do you think about...?"
  • "I read an interesting article about..."
  • "Have you seen the news about...?"

Be respectful of different opinions and perspectives.
